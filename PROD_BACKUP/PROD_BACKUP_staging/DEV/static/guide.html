1. Getting Started

Atlas is an intelligent learning platform that adapts to each student's skill level. It provides personalized diagnostics, structured expeditions (lessons), adaptive practice, and free-form chat tutoring across multiple territories (subjects).

How It Works

The learning flow follows a simple path:

  1. Take a Diagnostic — Atlas assesses your current knowledge with ~15-18 questions per territory.
  2. Get Personalized Expeditions — Based on your results, expeditions target your weakest areas first.
  3. Practice & Improve — Drill questions that automatically adjust to your difficulty level.
  4. Track Progress — See your growth through landmarks (badges), skill levels, and parent dashboard reports.
💡
Start with a diagnostic assessment in your first territory — this unlocks personalized expeditions and sets the right difficulty level for practice.

2. Student Login & Navigation

Students log in with their name and 4-digit PIN on the Atlas login page. After logging in, you'll see My Map — the home screen with all available territories (subjects).

Navigation

The sidebar (or bottom nav on mobile) has these sections:

  • 🧭 My Map — Your main screen with territories and quick access to diagnostics
  • 📊 Diagnostics — View diagnostic scores and skill levels per topic
  • 📖 Expeditions — Access structured lessons (unlocked after completing a diagnostic)
  • 🎯 Practice — Drill questions with adaptive difficulty
ℹ️
The ? button in the bottom-right corner gives you quick access to the interactive tour, help page, and this training guide at any time.

3. Diagnostic Assessments

Diagnostics are the foundation of personalized learning. Each diagnostic assesses your knowledge across all topics in a subject.

How Diagnostics Work

  • Each diagnostic asks approximately 15-18 questions spanning all topics in the subject
  • Questions adapt based on your responses — answer correctly and they get harder
  • You receive a skill rating for each topic: Needs Work, Developing, Proficient, or Advanced
  • Results are saved and visible on both the student Results page and parent dashboard

Taking a Diagnostic

  1. From the Home screen, find the "Diagnostic Assessments" section at the bottom
  2. Click Start Diagnostic on any subject
  3. Answer each question by typing your response in the chat input
  4. When the diagnostic completes, you'll see a summary of your scores per topic
💡
Don't worry about getting perfect scores — the diagnostic is designed to find areas where you can improve, so lessons can target those areas.

4. Expeditions (Lessons)

Expeditions become available after completing a diagnostic. They focus on topics where you scored lowest, helping you build up your weakest areas.

The 5-Step Expedition Structure

  1. Hook — An engaging introduction that connects the topic to something interesting or relatable
  2. Concept — Core teaching with clear explanations, examples, and analogies
  3. Guided Practice — Work through problems together with the tutor providing support
  4. Independent Practice — Try problems on your own with feedback after each attempt
  5. Wrap Up — Summary of what you learned and what to explore next

Navigating Expeditions

  • Click the Expeditions tab in the sidebar
  • If you've completed diagnostics in multiple territories, territory tabs appear at the top — click to switch between territories
  • Choose a topic — you'll see recommended topics based on your diagnostic results
  • The lesson moves through each step automatically
  • Step indicators at the top show your progress through the lesson

5. Practice Mode

Practice mode lets you drill questions on any territory. The difficulty adapts in real time based on your performance. If you've completed diagnostics in multiple territories, territory tabs appear at the top so you can switch between them.

How Difficulty Adapts

  • 3+ correct in a row → difficulty increases
  • 2+ wrong in a row → difficulty decreases
  • Available levels: Easy → Medium → Hard → Challenge

Using Hints

If you're stuck on a question, click the Hint button. The tutor will give you a helpful nudge without revealing the full answer. Using hints doesn't count against your score.

Ending Practice

Click End Practice when you're done. You'll see a summary showing your accuracy, questions answered, difficulty progression, and any badges earned.

⚠️
Your practice score only counts questions you actually answered — ending practice early won't penalize you for unanswered questions.

6. Study Mode (Flashcards & Quiz)

Study mode provides two quick review activities — flashcards and timed quizzes — powered by AI and personalized to each student's diagnostic results.

Accessing Study Mode

After completing a diagnostic, a 🃏 Study button appears in the sidebar. Click it to open the study picker where you choose a mode and topic.

Flashcards

The AI generates a set of 10 flashcards for your chosen topic. Each card shows a question on the front — tap or click to flip and reveal the answer on the back.

  • Got It — Mark a card as learned and move on
  • Study Again — The card goes back into the deck for another pass

At the end, you see a summary with your retention percentage and earn 20 XP for completing the session.

Quick Quiz

A timed multiple-choice quiz with 8 questions. Each question has 4 choices, and you get instant feedback (correct/incorrect + explanation) after each answer.

  • A running timer tracks how long you take
  • Progress bar shows how far through the quiz you are
  • Earn 25 XP for completing a quiz, plus a 40 XP bonus for a perfect score
💡
Flashcards are great for quick daily review. Quizzes are perfect for testing yourself before a real test. Both activities calibrate to your diagnostic level on each topic — harder if you scored well, easier if you need more practice.

7. Chat Tutoring

Beyond structured expeditions and practice, you can have free-form conversations with your Atlas guide about any topic in your territories.

Starting a Chat

  • Click any territory button on My Map
  • Type your question or topic in the message input
  • Your Atlas guide responds with explanations tailored to your grade level and knowledge

Chat is great for asking "why" questions, exploring curiosity, getting extra help on confusing concepts, or working through homework problems step by step.

8. Landmarks & Achievements

Earn landmarks (badges) as you progress through your learning journey! Landmarks appear as toast notifications when earned and are displayed in your profile and on the parent dashboard.

Landmark Types

  • 🌟 First Steps — Complete your first diagnostic
  • 📊 Assessment Pro — Complete diagnostics in multiple territories
  • 📚 Expedition Learner — Complete structured expeditions
  • 🎯 Practice Makes Perfect — Complete practice sessions
  • 🔥 Expedition Streak — Answer several questions correctly in a row
  • ⭐ Mastery landmarks — Reach Proficient or Advanced in topics

10. Parent Dashboard

The parent dashboard provides a comprehensive view of your child's Atlas learning journey. Access it by navigating to /parent (or click the parent link on the student login page).

Dashboard Tabs

  • 📊 Overview — Key metrics (total sessions, subjects active, average score, badges earned), 30-day activity calendar, subject breakdown radar chart, skill gap report, session history, and badges earned
  • 🔬 Diagnostics — Schedule, view results, or reset diagnostics per subject
  • ⚙️ Customization — Toggle subjects, add custom subjects, branding, grade level, feature flags
  • 💬 Feedback — Review student feedback, manage status, submit parent feedback
  • 🔗 Invites — Create and manage invite links to share with other families
  • 🔐 Settings — Change parent PIN, add students, manage student PINs

Switching Between Students

Use the dropdown selector at the top of the dashboard to switch between your children. Each student's data is kept completely separate.

ℹ️
The default parent PIN is 0000. Change it immediately in Settings to keep your dashboard secure.

11. Customization

Parents can extensively customize Atlas to match their family's needs.

Territory Management

  • Toggle territories — Enable or disable any built-in territory (Math, Science, English, History, Spanish)
  • Custom territories — Create entirely new territories with a name, icon, color, and 3-8 topics

Branding

  • Change the app title displayed in the header
  • Set a custom accent color to personalize the look

Grade Level & Features

  • Set the default grade level (6th - 9th grade) — this adjusts content difficulty
  • Toggle features on/off: Diagnostics, Lessons, Practice

Teaching Mode (Per Student)

At the bottom of the Customization tab, you'll find per-student teaching settings that apply to whichever student is currently selected in the dashboard.

  • Socratic Mode (default: ON) — When enabled, the tutor guides students to discover answers through questions rather than giving direct explanations. This applies to Chat and Lessons only — diagnostics and practice are unaffected. Toggle it off if a student needs more direct instruction.
💡
Socratic mode is set per student, so you can have it on for one child and off for another. The setting takes effect immediately — no need to restart anything.

12. PIN & Account Management

Parent PIN

The parent PIN protects the dashboard. To change it, go to Settings, enter your current PIN, then set and confirm a new 4-digit PIN.

Student PINs

Each student has their own 4-digit PIN for logging in. Parents can view all student PINs and reset them from the Settings tab. Students cannot change their own PINs — only parents can do this.

Adding a New Student

  1. Go to Settings in the parent dashboard
  2. Under "Add Student", enter the student's name
  3. Set a 4-digit PIN for them
  4. Choose an avatar
  5. Click "Add Student" — they'll appear in the student selector immediately

13. Invite Links

Share Atlas with other families by generating invite links. When someone uses your invite link, they'll be guided through the full setup process to create their own Atlas instance.

Creating an Invite

  1. Go to the Invites tab in the parent dashboard
  2. Optionally add a label (e.g., "For the Johnsons") to help you remember who it's for
  3. Set a max uses limit — choose unlimited, or restrict to 1, 3, 5, 10, or 25 uses
  4. Set an expiration — choose never, or auto-expire after 1, 3, 7, 14, or 30 days
  5. Click Generate Invite Link and share the link with the other family

What the Recipient Sees

When someone opens your invite link, they'll see the standard setup form with a banner showing your family name as the inviter. They complete the same 4-step setup (family name, student info, subjects, goals) and get their own fully independent tutor instance.

Managing Invites

  • Status badges — Each invite shows its current status: Active, Expired, Max Reached, or Revoked
  • Usage tracking — See how many times each invite has been used and which families signed up with it
  • Copy link — Re-copy any active invite link to share again
  • Revoke — Permanently disable an invite so it can't be used anymore
ℹ️
Each family that signs up via an invite gets their own completely separate instance — they cannot see your data and you cannot see theirs.

14. Content Safety

Atlas enforces multiple layers of content safety to keep every interaction age-appropriate and academically focused. These rules are built into the AI's instructions and cannot be bypassed by students.

AI Content Rules

The following rules are enforced on every AI response across all modes (chat, lessons, diagnostics, practice, flashcards, and quizzes):

  1. Age-appropriate content only — All content is calibrated to the student's grade level. Sensitive academic topics (human reproduction, historical violence, substance abuse) use only clinical/scientific terminology at a textbook level. No graphic descriptions, slang, or innuendo.
  2. No inappropriate content — The AI will never produce content that is sexual, violent, profane, or otherwise inappropriate for a minor.
  3. Subject-focused redirection — If a student asks about something outside their current subject, the tutor gently redirects them back to the topic.
  4. Role-play and manipulation protection — If a student tries to get the AI to act as a different character, ignore its rules, or leave the academic context, it politely declines.
  5. Prompt protection — The AI will never reveal its internal instructions or system prompt, even if asked directly.
  6. Language standards — If a student uses inappropriate language, the tutor redirects without repeating it.
  7. No personal or medical advice — The tutor sticks strictly to academics and will not provide personal, medical, or non-academic guidance.
  8. Conservative by default — When in doubt about whether content is appropriate, the AI errs on the side of caution.

Pre-Screening Protections

Before a student's message even reaches the AI, it passes through two layers of input screening:

  • Prompt injection detection — 15 regex patterns detect attempts to manipulate the AI (e.g., "ignore your instructions," "pretend to be," "system prompt"). These are blocked immediately with a friendly redirect.
  • Blocked topic filter — Explicitly harmful topics (weapons, drugs, self-harm, explicit content) are blocked outright and never sent to the AI.

Logging

All blocked events (both injection attempts and blocked topics) are logged with timestamps in the safety log. Every student conversation turn is also logged to a per-student conversation log file for parent review.

For parents: The safety log is available via the API at /api/safety-log. A future update will surface this data directly in the parent dashboard so you can review safety events without technical tools.

15. Grade Scaling (Grades 6–12)

Atlas supports students from 6th grade through 12th grade. All expeditions, diagnostics, practice sessions, flashcards, and quizzes automatically adapt to the student's current grade level.

How It Works

  • Grade-aware prompts — The AI adjusts vocabulary, complexity, and teaching style based on grade level. Younger students (grades 6–7) get simpler language, more encouragement, and concrete examples. Older students (grades 11–12) get academic rigor, college-level vocabulary, and more independent problem-solving.
  • Grade-specific topics — Each subject has a full set of topics appropriate for the student's grade. For example, Math grade 6 covers ratios and basic geometry, while Math grade 11 covers trigonometry and pre-calculus.
  • Grade-aware safety rules — Content safety rules adapt to the student's age range (e.g., grade 6 = ages 10–12, grade 12 = ages 16–18).
  • Grade progression — Parents can change a student's grade from the student header in the parent dashboard. The grade dropdown appears next to the student's name.
Note: Changing a student's grade immediately affects all new lessons, diagnostics, and practice sessions. Existing completed work (past diagnostics, lesson history) is preserved regardless of grade changes.

16. Adaptive Learning Engine

Atlas uses a smart adaptive engine that tracks your mastery across every topic and automatically adjusts what to study next.

How It Works

Each topic gets a blended mastery score (0–100%) computed from three signals:

  • Diagnostic score (40%) — Your initial assessment result
  • Practice accuracy (45%) — How you perform on practice questions for that topic
  • Lesson completions (15%) — Bonus credit for completing lessons on the topic

Spaced Repetition

The engine schedules review sessions based on your mastery tier. Topics you know well are reviewed less often, while weak topics come back sooner:

  • Needs Work (0–39%) — Review daily
  • Developing (40–64%) — Review every 3 days
  • Proficient (65–84%) — Review weekly
  • Advanced (85–100%) — Review every 2 weeks

Study Plan

On the home screen, students see a prioritized study plan with up to 5 recommended topics. The plan ranks items by urgency:

  • Weak topics that are also due for review (highest priority)
  • Topics due for spaced repetition review
  • Weak topics not yet due (proactive strengthening)
  • Proficient topics that could be pushed to advanced

Clicking a study plan item takes students directly to the appropriate lesson or practice session.

Adaptive Difficulty

When starting a practice session, the difficulty (easy/medium/hard) is now set from the blended mastery score rather than just the diagnostic. As students practice more, the difficulty adapts to their actual performance.

Parent Dashboard

Parents can find the Adaptive Learning section under the 📈 Analytics tab on the parent dashboard, showing per-subject mastery breakdowns, which topics are due for review, and the recommended study plan.

Tip:
The more your student practices, the smarter the engine becomes. Diagnostic scores are the starting point, but practice accuracy has the highest weight — consistent effort improves mastery scores even if the initial diagnostic was low.

17. Standards Alignment

Atlas maps student progress to nationally recognized and state-specific academic standards, giving parents and students a clear picture of how skills align with grade-level expectations.

What's Covered

Standards are mapped across all four core subjects for grades 6–12:

  • Math & ELA — Common Core State Standards (CCSS). Topics map to specific domain codes like 8.EE, RL.8, HSF-IF, etc.
  • Science — Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS). Topics map to Performance Expectations in Physical Science (PS), Life Science (LS), Earth & Space Science (ESS), and Engineering (ETS).
  • Social Studies — State-specific standards loaded based on the state set during family setup. For Georgia, this uses the Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) with domain codes like SS8H5, SSCG1, SSUSH17, etc.

Custom subjects (e.g., Latin, Spanish) do not have mapped standards frameworks and are noted as such in the report.

How Mastery Is Calculated

Standards mastery comes from the same blended score used by the adaptive engine — diagnostic results (40%), practice accuracy (45%), and lesson completions (15%). Each domain shows an aggregated mastery percentage based on the topics that map to it.

Parent Dashboard View

The parent dashboard's 📈 Analytics tab includes a Standards Alignment section showing:

  • Overall mastery per subject with tier labels (Needs Work → Advanced)
  • The framework name displayed per subject (e.g., "Common Core", "NGSS", "Georgia Standards of Excellence")
  • Number of standards assessed vs. total standards for the grade
  • Per-domain breakdown with mastery bars and expandable standard details
  • Each standard code linked to its source topic for transparency

Student View

Students can access their standards progress via the 📏 Standards button in the sidebar (appears after completing a diagnostic in any core subject). This shows a simplified domain-level view with mastery scores for all assessed subjects.

Grade Configuration

Each instance can configure a grade range (default 6–12). The standards displayed match the student's grade level. For example, a Grade 8 student sees 8.EE (Math), RL.8 (ELA), MS-PS (Science), and SS8H5 (Social Studies), while a Grade 10 student sees HSG-CO, RL.9-10, HS-PS, and SSWH1 respectively.

State Selection

During family setup, parents select their state. This determines which state-specific standards file is loaded for Social Studies. Currently Georgia (GSE) is fully supported. Additional state mappings can be added as JSON files in the data/state_standards/ directory.

Note:
Custom subjects (Latin, Spanish, etc.) do not have national or state standards frameworks and are excluded from the standards report. If your state's Social Studies standards are not yet available, the section will note this.

18. Progress Checkpoints & Growth Tracking

Progress Checkpoints are periodic reassessments that track student growth over time. Unlike the initial diagnostic (which covers all topics), checkpoints are shorter, focused assessments that only test topics where the student still has room to improve.

How It Works

  • Auto-scheduled — Checkpoints become due on a configurable interval (default: every 2 weeks) after the student's last diagnostic or checkpoint
  • Focused scope — Only topics scoring below 90% mastery are included; fully mastered topics are skipped
  • Shorter format — 6-10 questions per checkpoint (vs. ~18 for a full diagnostic)
  • All enabled subjects — Any subject with a completed diagnostic and weak topics is eligible

Student Experience

When a checkpoint is due, a blue banner appears on the student's home screen: "📈 Progress Checkpoint Ready!" with the subjects listed. The student clicks "Start Checkpoint" to begin. The flow works just like a diagnostic — answer questions in the chat, and results are recorded when complete. After finishing, a Growth Summary appears showing score changes per topic, with level-up celebrations for any tier improvements.

Parent Controls

Parents manage checkpoints from the 📈 Analytics tab on their dashboard under Growth Tracking:

  • Adjust interval — Change from 1 to 8 weeks between checkpoints
  • Snooze — Delay a due checkpoint by 3 or 7 days
  • Enable/Disable — Turn periodic checkpoints on or off entirely
  • Trigger Now — Mark a checkpoint as due immediately so the student sees it on their next login

Growth Charts

The Analytics tab displays per-subject growth timelines showing the progression from the initial diagnostic through each checkpoint. Each entry shows the date, score, and per-topic deltas (improvements or declines). Level-ups (tier changes like "Developing → Proficient") are highlighted with celebration indicators.

Note:
Checkpoint results merge back into the student's profile, updating their mastery scores. This means lessons, practice, and study plans all adapt based on the latest checkpoint results — not just the original diagnostic.

20. Admin Dashboard

The Admin Dashboard is a management interface for platform administrators to oversee all family instances and manage feedback across the system. Access it by navigating to /admin. No PIN is required — the admin dashboard is a separate authentication system.

Instance Management

The primary tab displays all family instances in a comprehensive table with the following columns:

  • Name — The family's name
  • Students — Number of students in the instance
  • Last Active — When the instance was last used
  • Created — When the instance was set up
  • Feedback Count — Total feedback items submitted
  • Status — Current status badge (Active, Inactive, etc.)

Search & Filter

Use the search bar to filter instances by:

  • Family name — Full or partial match
  • Email — Parent email address
  • Instance ID — Unique instance identifier
  • Status — Filter by Active or Inactive

Instance Details

Click any instance row to open the full details view, which includes:

  • Configuration — Instance settings (subjects, grade level, feature flags)
  • Students List — All students with their login PINs
  • Topics — Enabled subjects and their topics

PIN Management

From the instance details view, administrators can:

  • Reset Parent PIN — Generate a new parent PIN for the family (the default becomes 0000)
  • Reset Student PIN — Generate a new PIN for any student

Instance Status Control

Administrators can manage instance availability:

  • Deactivate instances — Disable an instance temporarily or permanently (families cannot access their tutor)
  • Reactivate instances — Re-enable a previously deactivated instance
  • Delete instances — Permanently remove an instance from the platform. Click the red 🗑️ Delete button in the Actions column. You will be asked to confirm twice before deletion proceeds. This removes the instance from the registry and cleans up all associated data (students, conversations, feedback, settings). The default instance cannot be deleted.

Quick Actions

Each instance detail view includes quick-access buttons:

  • 📱 Open Student View — Launch the student login page for this instance (useful for testing)
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Open Parent Dashboard — Access the parent dashboard (requires you to enter the parent PIN)

Feedback Management

Switch to the Feedback tab to manage feedback across all instances.

Unified Feedback Inbox

The feedback tab displays a unified inbox of all feedback submissions from all family instances.

Feedback Filters

Filter feedback by:

  • Title or content — Search keyword in feedback text
  • Family name — Which family submitted the feedback
  • Type — Category (Bug Report, Feature Request, Content Issue, General)
  • Status — Unresolved, Resolved, Acknowledged, Declined

Feedback Status Tracking

Feedback items display status badges showing:

  • Unresolved — Feedback waiting for action (shown with unresolved count in header)
  • Resolved — Completed and closed
  • Acknowledged — Received and reviewed
  • Declined — Not actionable or rejected

Feedback Actions

Administrators can:

  • Reply to feedback — Send a message back to the family with updates or questions
  • Mark as Resolved — Close the feedback item after addressing it
  • Mark as Acknowledged — Confirm receipt without immediate resolution
  • Mark as Declined — Indicate the feedback cannot be acted upon

Student Oversight

From any instance detail view, click the 📊 Overview button next to a student to open their full academic profile. This gives administrators a read-only view of a student's progress without needing to log in as the student or parent.

Student Overview Details

The student oversight panel displays:

  • Profile Header — Avatar, name, grade, XP, level, PIN, activity days, and join date
  • Stats Cards — Total diagnostics completed, lessons taken, practice sessions, and overall accuracy
  • Subject Mastery — Color-coded progress bars for each subject (green for 70%+, yellow for 40%+, red below 40%)
  • Skill Gaps — Topics where the student needs additional support
  • Growth History — Session-by-session progress with score deltas
  • Recent Activity — Last 10 sessions with dates and types
  • Conversation Log — Recent tutor interactions showing questions and responses

Student Quick Actions

From the student overview, administrators can:

  • Open Student View — Launch the student's login page directly
  • Back to Instance — Return to the family instance detail

Invite Management

The Invites tab lets administrators manage all invite codes across the platform. Switch to the Invites tab to see a unified view of every invite code.

Viewing Invites

Each invite card displays:

  • Label — A descriptive name for the invite
  • Status — Active, Revoked, Expired, or Max Reached
  • Code — The unique invite code
  • Uses — How many times it has been used vs. the maximum allowed
  • Expiration — When the invite expires (or "Never" for no expiration)
  • Creator — Which family or admin created the invite
  • Used By — Which families were created using this invite
  • Invite Link — A clickable/copyable URL to share with new families

Creating Invites

Click the + New Invite button to create a new invite code. You can set:

  • Label — An optional description (e.g., "Spring 2026 cohort")
  • Max Uses — Limit how many families can use it (0 = unlimited)
  • Expiration — How many days before it expires (0 = never)

Revoking Invites

Click the Revoke button on any active invite to disable it immediately. Existing families created from that invite are not affected — only new signups are blocked.

Safety & Moderation

The Safety tab aggregates all safety events across every family instance into a single moderation view. Safety events are generated when the content safety system blocks a message due to a prohibited topic or detects a prompt injection attempt.

Viewing Safety Events

Each safety event card displays:

  • Severity — Color-coded badge: 🔴 High (blocked topics like weapons/drugs), 🟡 Medium (injection attempts), or 🟢 Low (other)
  • Type — "Blocked Topic" or "Injection Attempt" label
  • Student & Family — The student name and family instance that triggered the event
  • Message — The original message that was flagged
  • Matched Pattern — The safety rule pattern that triggered the block
  • Timestamp — When the event occurred

Filtering Safety Events

Use the search bar to filter events by student name, family name, or message content. The severity and type dropdown filters let you quickly isolate high-priority events or focus on a specific category.

Admin Notes

Add notes to any safety event by typing in the note field and clicking Add Note (or pressing Enter). Notes are timestamped and persist across sessions, making it easy to track review status and follow-up actions.

Conversation Context

Click the 💬 Context button on any event to expand a panel showing the surrounding conversation — messages from both the student and the AI tutor around the time of the safety event. This helps you understand the full context of what happened.

Usage Analytics

The Analytics tab provides platform-wide metrics and engagement data. Click the Analytics tab to load a comprehensive view of how the tutor is being used.

Analytics Overview

The analytics dashboard displays:

  • Overview Cards — Active instances, total students, students active in the last 7 days, sessions this week, and total sessions
  • Session Breakdown — Total lessons completed, practice sessions, and diagnostics taken across the platform
  • Activity Trend — A 30-day bar chart showing daily student activity levels, making it easy to spot usage patterns and drops
  • Subject Popularity — Color-coded bars ranking subjects by total activity, with a breakdown of diagnostics (D), lessons (L), and practice (P) for each
  • Inactive Students — A list of students who haven't been active in 7+ days, sorted by how long they've been away, with their family name for context

Click Refresh Analytics at the bottom to reload the data at any time.

System Health

The System tab provides operational visibility into the platform. Click the System tab to view server status, API usage, and error logs.

System Health Details

The system health dashboard displays:

  • Server Status — Green/red indicator, uptime duration, AI model in use, and overall error rate
  • API Usage — Total API calls, calls today, calls this week, and average response time
  • Token & Cost Tracking — Input/output token counts and estimated costs (today and all-time)
  • API Call Trend — A 14-day bar chart showing daily API call volume
  • Storage — Data directory size, total file count, and number of instances
  • Recent Errors — A log of the most recent server errors with timestamps, error types, and messages
💡
Use the Analytics tab to monitor engagement and the System tab to monitor operational health. Together, they give you full visibility into both how the tutor is being used and whether it's running smoothly.

21. Tips & Tricks

For Students

  • Start with diagnostics — They unlock personalized expeditions and set practice to the right level
  • Use hints wisely — In practice mode, hints help you learn without just giving the answer
  • Ask "why" — In chat mode, asking why things work the way they do leads to deeper understanding
  • Practice regularly — Short daily sessions are more effective than long cramming sessions
  • Explore different territories — Atlas covers Math, Science, English, History, and Spanish (plus any custom territories)

For Parents

  • Check the activity calendar — See at a glance which days your child was active
  • Review skill gaps — The gap report highlights exactly which topics need attention
  • Use custom subjects — Add topics your child is studying at school for extra practice
  • Change the default PIN — Keep your dashboard secure by changing from 0000
  • Read feedback — Your child's feedback can reveal what they find helpful or frustrating
  • Share with other families — Use invite links to bring other families onboard. You can track who signed up from the Invites tab

22. Troubleshooting

Common Issues

  • "Unknown subject" error — This can happen if a custom subject was deleted. Try refreshing the page.
  • Diagnostic doesn't start — Make sure diagnostics are enabled in the parent Customization tab.
  • Expeditions not showing — Expeditions require a completed diagnostic in that territory first. If you've completed multiple diagnostics, use the territory tabs at the top of the Expeditions or Practice page to switch territories.
  • PIN not working — Double-check you're entering the right PIN. Parents can reset student PINs from Settings.
  • Server error — Check that the server is running. Try refreshing the page after a few seconds.
