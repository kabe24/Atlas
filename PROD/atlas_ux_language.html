Atlas

UX Language Mapping — Hybrid Approach

Hybrid rule: Student-facing surfaces use Atlas exploration language. Parent and teacher dashboards keep traditional academic metrics. When in doubt, clarity wins over brand consistency.

What Parents & Teachers See

Parent and teacher dashboards keep traditional academic language: grade levels, percentages, scores, completion rates. These audiences need precision, not brand flavor. The only Atlas touches on parent/teacher surfaces are visual (colors, fonts, logo).
SurfaceLanguageRationale
Parent Overview tab Traditional (grade level, % complete, scores) Parents need clear academic metrics
Parent Diagnostics tab Traditional (grade-level benchmarks, skill gaps) Clinical accuracy matters for assessment
Parent Analytics tab Traditional (sessions, time spent, scores over time) Data-driven parents want real numbers
Teacher Dashboard Traditional (same as parent) Teachers expect standard educational reporting
Parent Help & FAQ Traditional with brief Atlas context "Your child's 'expedition' is what they see as a lesson"
